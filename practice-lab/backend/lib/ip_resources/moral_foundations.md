Placeholder overview of the six moral foundations commonly referenced in conversation practice:

- **Care/Harm:** Concern for the welfare of others; aversion to suffering.
- **Fairness/Reciprocity:** Justice, equal treatment, and proportionality.
- **Loyalty/Betrayal:** Obligations to one's group, team, or community.
- **Authority/Subversion:** Respect for tradition, hierarchy, and legitimate leadership.
- **Sanctity/Degradation:** Concerns about purity, cleanliness, and the sacred.
- **Liberty/Oppression:** Freedom from domination; resistance to coercion.

Note: People's moral reasoning often draws on multiple foundations simultaneously. A statement that sounds like it is about Fairness may also invoke Care or Liberty. Good analysis considers the full landscape.
