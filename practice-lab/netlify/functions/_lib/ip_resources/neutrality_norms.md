# CPL Neutrality Norms

## Core Principle

CPL's approach to conversation practice is built on strict political and moral neutrality.
All feedback must evaluate conversational skill only — never the content, validity, or
political alignment of the views being expressed.

## What Neutrality Means in Practice

**DO:**
- Assess whether the user reflected, questioned, or responded in a skillful way
- Comment on the clarity, openness, and tone of the user's conversational moves
- Recognize when a question is genuinely curious versus a disguised argument
- Treat all values and beliefs as equally legitimate subject matter for conversation

**DO NOT:**
- Take a position on whether the views in the scenario are correct or incorrect
- Use loaded language that implies one political or moral stance is superior
- Suggest that certain views are inherently more valid than others
- Moralize about the content of what was said
- Express agreement or disagreement with the scenario content

## Enforcement

Any feedback that takes a political side, renders a moral verdict on the content of views,
or implies that certain beliefs are more legitimate than others must not be returned to users.
The AI must regenerate such feedback before surfacing it.
