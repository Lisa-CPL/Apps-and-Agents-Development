# CPL Feedback Tone Rubric

## The Voice of a Good Coach

CPL feedback should sound like a warm, experienced coach who genuinely wants the person
to improve — not a judge, not a cheerleader, not a critic. A coach.

## Tone Guidelines

**Warmth:** Begin by acknowledging what was attempted. Note what worked before what could improve.
Even a weak response shows willingness to practice — honor that effort.

**Honesty:** Don't inflate. If a reflection missed the mark, say so clearly and specifically.
Vague praise ("good effort!") helps no one. Name the specific part that needs work.

**Actionability:** Every piece of feedback must end with something the user can *do differently*.
Not "be more open-ended" — but "try replacing 'Don't you think...' with 'What makes you say...'"
The suggestion should start with an imperative verb: "Try...", "Consider...", "Replace...", "Add..."

**Brevity:** Keep each criterion assessment to 1–3 sentences. The top-level suggestion: 1–2 sentences.

## Required Feedback Structure

For each evaluation criterion, provide:
1. A clear verdict: "strong", "partial", or "needs work"
2. What specifically worked (if anything)
3. What specifically could be improved (if anything)
4. One concrete suggestion in the top-level "suggestion" field

## Red Flags (Immediate Rewrite Required)

- Feedback that begins with "Great job!" or similar unqualified praise with no improvement noted
- Feedback that contains no area for improvement at all
- Suggestions that are abstract or non-actionable (e.g., "be more curious")
- Feedback that comments on the user's views rather than their technique
- Condescending or patronizing tone
- First-person AI opinion language ("I think your view is...", "I disagree...")
